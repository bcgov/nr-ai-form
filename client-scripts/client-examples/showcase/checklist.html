BC Housing Permit Application Checklist

1 Applicant Information

  • Full legal name
  • Email and phone number
  • Mailing address
  • Proof of ownership or letter of authorization

2 Property Information

  • Civic address of the property
  • Property ID (PID)
  • Legal description (lot, block, plan)
  • Zoning designation
  • Site-specific notes (slopes, easements, floodplain)

3 Project Details

  • Type of work: new construction, renovation, addition, demolition, other
  • Detailed project description
  • Estimated project value (CAD)
  • Number of floors / total square footage (if applicable)
  • Renovation type or size of addition
  • Reason for demolition (if applicable)

4 Contractor Information

  • Contractor’s name
  • Contractor license number
  • Contact information
  • Insurance and WorkSafeBC registration (if required)

5 Plans & Drawings

  • Site plan / plot plan (scaled, shows boundaries, setbacks)
  • Floor plans / architectural drawings
  • Structural drawings (if required)
  • Electrical, plumbing, mechanical drawings (if applicable)
  • Other supporting plans (landscape, retaining walls, secondary suites)

6 Reports & Supporting Documents

  • Geotechnical report (hillside or waterfront)
  • Arborist report (if trees affected)
  • Environmental assessment (if required)
  • Letters of authorization
  • Other documents requested by municipality

7 Document Submission

  • All plans in correct format (PDF, CAD, JPG/PNG)
  • File sizes meet municipality requirements
  • Required copies included (digital / hard copy)

8 Fees

  • Calculate permit fees based on project type, value, and size
  • Payment method prepared (online, cheque, in-person)

9 Declaration

  • Read and understand declaration statement
  • Check agreement checkbox or sign form

10 Review & Submission

  • Double-check all sections for accuracy
  • Verify property identifiers (PID, legal description, address)
  • Confirm all project-specific fields are filled
  • Submit according to municipality instructions

11 Post-Submission

  • Keep a copy of the application and all documents
  • Track application status
  • Respond promptly to municipal staff requests
